Why video diffusion attention is a different beast from LLM decode

The conceptual shift is from “read a growing KV cache efficiently” to “avoid recomputing huge bidirectional 3D attention maps across many denoising steps.”

LLM decode attention

▸Structure: causal, autoregressive.
▸Dataflow: one/few query tokens attend to cached K/V.
▸Common bottleneck: KV-cache bandwidth and scheduler/batching interactions.
▸Sparse idea: skip KV blocks or exploit long-context locality.

Video DiT diffusion attention

▸Structure: mostly bidirectional 3D latent attention.
▸Dataflow: Q/K/V change every denoising step.
▸Common bottleneck: repeated full attention over frame × height × width tokens.
▸Sparse idea: exploit spatial/temporal locality, head specialization, block patterns, timestep redundancy, or semantic clustering.

The useful warning

FLOP sparsity is not wall-clock sparsity. If selected K/V blocks are scattered, you can save theoretical work but lose tensor-core efficiency, coalescing, occupancy, or load balance. That is why token permutation, block lists, and hardware-aware kernels are central.

Speedup ≠ dense_FLOPs / sparse_FLOPs Speedup ≈ f(mask_cost, metadata_loads, block_contiguity, tensor_core_util, load_balance, quality_budget)

Taxonomy: what changes weights, and what is plug-and-play?

For a Baseten-style model performance ramp, separate methods by deployment friction. Training-free methods are better for quick production experiments; trainable methods define the frontier but require model adaptation and validation.

STA SVG SVG2 PISA SVOO NABLA VSA SALAD Sparge2 BLADE Turbo MonarchRT

Post-training / inference-time

STA, SVG, SVG2, AdaSpa, Sparse-vDiT, PISA, SVOO, PASA, NABLA, SpargeAttention, XAttention. Best first experiments.

Hybrid

Radial Attention can be used on pretrained models for speed, but long-video extension uses LoRA-style finetuning.

Requires training or finetuning

VSA, SALAD, SpargeAttention2, Sparse Forcing, MonarchRT. Better for learning the frontier than for immediate drop-in serving.

Full-stack acceleration

BLADE and TurboDiffusion combine attention changes with step distillation and/or quantization. Very important baselines, but not pure kernel papers.

Method map: papers and what each teaches

Numbers below are self-reported and not directly comparable across papers because models, GPUs, resolutions, timesteps, and baselines differ. Treat them as a reading/benchmarking guide, not a leaderboard.

Training-free / post-training first

STA

no training

Sliding Tile Attention: local spatiotemporal tile windows plus a hardware-aware kernel. The cleanest “sparse video kernel” starting point.

Study for: how to make 2D/3D local attention hardware-efficient rather than just masking dense attention.

SVG / Sparse VideoGen

no training

Classifies heads into spatial vs temporal sparse patterns with online profiling, tensor layout transforms, and custom kernels.

head specializationonline profilinglayout transform
Study for: connecting attention-map structure to runtime layout and kernel design.

AdaSpa

no training

Dynamic blockified sparse patterns with online precise search; exploits timestep stability of sparse patterns in long video generation.

dynamic maskspattern reuseHunyuanVideo
Study for: deciding when mask/search overhead amortizes across diffusion timesteps.

Sparse-vDiT

offline profiling

Finds diagonal, multi-diagonal, and vertical-stripe sparsity patterns and uses pattern-optimized sparse kernels.

offline searchstructured patternsWan/Hunyuan/CogVideoX
Study for: layer/head-specific pattern choice and the gap between theoretical FLOP cuts and actual speedups.

SVG2

no training

Uses semantic-aware token clustering/permutation so critical tokens become more contiguous and GPU-friendly.

k-meanspermutationcontiguity
Study for: the core kernel lesson that sparse tokens must be packed, not scattered.

PISA

no training

Piecewise Sparse Attention computes critical blocks exactly and approximates non-critical blocks instead of dropping them outright.

exact + approximateTaylor approxquality preservation
Study for: high-sparsity quality preservation and the “do less work without deleting all context” design pattern.

SVOO

no training

Offline layer-wise sensitivity profiling plus online bidirectional Q/K co-clustering; recent and strong as a training-free baseline.

layer heterogeneityQK co-clusteringWan2.1
Study for: separating intrinsic per-layer pruning budgets from online input-dependent clustering.

NABLA

no custom kernel first

Neighborhood-Adaptive Block-Level Attention builds per-head sparse masks from pooled Q/K blocks and plugs into PyTorch FlexAttention.

FlexAttentionblock poolingSTA union
Study for: the fastest prototype path before writing a bespoke low-level sparse kernel.

PASA

no training

Targets temporal flicker from deterministic sparse routing using precision allocation, grouping, and stochastic selection bias.

temporal smoothnessrouting stabilitynewer
Study for: quality failure modes unique to video, not just attention-map reconstruction error.

SpargeAttention / XAttention

generic plug-in

General sparse attention frameworks: SpargeAttn combines sparse + quantized filtering; XAttention scores blocks using antidiagonal sums.

genericlong contextvideo evals
Study for: reusable sparse-attention ideas beyond video DiTs, but do not treat them as the most video-specific baselines.

What counts as a fair win?
1Compare against FA4 or best available dense attention, not a naive PyTorch baseline.
2Include mask generation, clustering, permutation, metadata, and memory movement in timing.
3Track quality: VBench, PSNR/SSIM/LPIPS where applicable, plus temporal flicker and motion smoothness.
4Compare against caching/broadcasting such as PAB, because it may win without block-sparse kernels.
5Measure actual latency and GPU counters, not just theoretical FLOP reduction.
Important 2026 watchlist: MonarchRT is worth knowing because it explicitly discusses real-time video diffusion, structured attention parameterization, custom Triton kernels, and B200 comparisons against FA4. I would keep it as a frontier paper, not the first ramp exercise.

Drop-in curriculum module after FA4

Keep this narrow. The goal is one benchmark report that says when sparse video attention beats dense FA-class baselines, and when overhead or quality risk erases the win.

Day A — Map the space

Read FA4 recap, STA, SVG2, PISA, SVOO or NABLA, VSA/BLADE, and PAB. Classify each by weight updates, mask type, kernel strategy, and quality metric.

Deliverable: 1-page taxonomy + baseline hierarchy.

Day B — Prototype masks

Use PyTorch FlexAttention or Triton to prototype 3D sliding tile, radial, random block-sparse, and top-k block masks over synthetic video latent shapes.

Deliverable: latency table including mask/search overhead.

Day C — Kernel systems pass

Implement or inspect one block-sparse forward path. Focus on block lists, memory layout, coalescing, and whether selected K/V blocks are contiguous.

Deliverable: profiler-backed memo: why speedup appeared or disappeared.

Optional Day D — Frontier

Read VSA, BLADE, SpargeAttention2, TurboDiffusion, or MonarchRT to understand trainable and full-stack speedup directions.

Decision guide: should sparse video attention enter your Baseten ramp?

Yes — but as a compact operator-design module, not the main curriculum spine.

Have you studied FA4 first?
If no, do FA4/CuTeDSL dense attention first. Sparse kernels make more sense once the dense baseline’s tiling, softmax, MMA pipeline, and Blackwell features are clear.
Do you have B200 access?
If yes, compare against FA4-backed dense/custom attention. If no, use H100/FA3 or FlexAttention/Triton as a proxy, but avoid overclaiming B200 results.
Do you want production-ready acceleration?
Start with training-free methods and caching/broadcasting baselines: STA/SVG2/PISA/SVOO/NABLA/PAB/SageAttention. Measure total wall-clock, not isolated math.
Do you want frontier research depth?
Read VSA, BLADE, SpargeAttention2, SALAD, TurboDiffusion, and MonarchRT. These teach model/operator co-design but require finetuning/distillation or custom model variants.
What should be the output?
A benchmark/profiler memo: dense baseline, sparse implementation, overhead accounting, quality risks, and a clear recommendation about whether sparse attention is worth production complexity.
